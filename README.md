<h1 align="center">Jacksonville History Center Presents: Echos of Cecil Field</h1>

# Introducing the Speaker
## Gayle Simpson Garrison
Gayle Simpson Garrison grew up as a Navy kid, moving around the country. She became interested in historyand political science while living in Northern Virginia. She spent her last two years of high school in Jacksonville before heading off to Samford University in Birmingham, Alabama where she majored in Political Science with a concentration in Public Administration.
After college, Gayle worked on Capitol Hill for Congressman Cliff Stearns. She married Sam Garrison in 1999, and in 2001 they moved back to Jacksonville after Sam finished law school. Gayle taught in the history department at St. Johns Country Day School in Orange Park for 16 years. During that time, she completed a Master of Arts Degree in American History from Gettysburg College through the Gilder Lehrman Institue for American History.
Her presentation for the Jacksonville History Center is based on her Master's Thesis, an oral history on the closure and redevelopment of Cecil and its impact on the City of Jacksonville. 

# Introducing the Panalists
## Alberta Hipps
An experienced government relations and business development professional, Alberta Hipps is a former Jacksonville City Council representative, servinf two terms 1995-2003 and recieving the Charles D. Webb Award for most effective City COuncil member. Alberta was City Council President during the 1999 transition of Cecil Field from military to civilian use, working with federal, regional and local authorities and community stakeholders to grow the city's land inventory by 17,000 acres. Prior to her political career, Alberta had a career in heathcare that ran from 1974 to 1997, primarily with St. Vincent's Health System. She has degrees from the Jacksonville institutions of higher learning: an associate's degree in nursing from FSCJ; a bachelor's in nursing from UNF, and a master's in business administration from JU.
## Ed Turner
Executive Director of the National POW/MIA Memorial and Museum, Ed Turner served in the U.S. Navy as a Naval Flight Officer for 31 years, flying over 4100 flight hours, making six overseas carrier deployments, including a combat mission in Desert Storm, before retiring with the rank of captain. His final assignment in the Navy was as the Commanding Officer of Naval Education and Training Security Assistance Field Activity. During his career, Ed earned a master’s degree from the Naval Command and Staff College in National Security and Strategic Studies.
## Robert Simpson
After graduating from Mississippi State University with a degree in Business Administration, Bob Simpson joined the U.S. Navy where he served for 24 years before his retirement as a Navy Captain. During his Navy career he attained designation both as a Naval Flight Officer and Surface Warfare Officer and held assignments in the Office of the Secretary of Defense and Naval Military Personnel Command, Executive Officer of Patrol Squadron 30, Commanding Officer of Patrol Squadron 56, and as the assistant navigator of the aircraft carrier U.S.S. Enterprise. His last assignment in the Navy was as Commander, Patrol and Reconnaissance Wing Eleven at Naval Air Station Jacksonville.
Bob’s career post-Navy focused on the conversion and redevelopment of Cecil Field from military to civilian use. Beginning in 1999, his positions included stints with the Mayor’s office, the Jacksonville Airport Authority, and as Cecil Field Airport Manager after it was transferred from the Navy to the Airport Authority. From 2006 to 2008, Bob served as the Airport Authority’s Director of Aviation Management for four airports, and was named Chief Operating Officer / Deputy Executive Director of the JAA in 2011.
## Matt Bocchino
Currently serving as director of Cecil Airport & Spaceport, Matt Bocchino’s 20-year career spans the spectrum of airport management, from general aviation to international airports, as well as a commercial spaceport. Prior to joining Cecil Airport, Matt held positions at Jacksonville International Airport, Herlong Recreational Airport, and Ft. Lauderdale Executive Airport. An active private pilot, Matt earned bachelor’s and master’s degrees from Jacksonville University.
